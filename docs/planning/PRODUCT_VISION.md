---

## How It Works

### 1. You Choose Your Sources (Not an Algorithm)
- Add YouTube channels you trust
- Add RSS feeds from blogs/news you like
- Preset packs for quick start ("Tech News", "Science", "Wholesome")
- No algorithmic suggestions (unless you explicitly ask)

### 2. Heavy Filtering by Default
- Block keywords (politics, drama, negativity)
- Filter by content length (only 5-10min videos, 3min articles)
- Filter by publish date (only fresh content)
- Source safety levels (mark sources as "always safe")

### 3. Engaging Feed Experience
- Reddit-style scrolling feed
- Mix of videos and articles
- Inline reading (no outlinks!)
- One-tap actions (save, dismiss, not now)
- Progress tracking (know how much is left)

### 4. ADHD-Friendly Design
- No context switching (everything in-app)
- Clear progress indicators
- Resume where you left off
- Gentle time boundaries (nudges, not hard stops)
- Celebration of completion ("Nice work! ğŸ‰")

### Primary Users
- **ADHD/neurodivergent folks** who struggle with traditional apps

- **Ex-X/Reddit users** who want the UX without the toxicity
- **Intentional content consumers** who value quality over quantity

### Use Cases
- **Morning routine:** Read curated news without politics
- **Break time:** Watch a quick video without falling into rabbit hole
- **Deep dive:** Read long-form articles with progress tracking
- **Wind down:** Wholesome content before bed (no doom-scrolling)
